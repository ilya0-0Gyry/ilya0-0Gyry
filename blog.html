GENERAL CHARACTERISTICS: Ideal Burmese should create a general impression of a medium-sized cat with strong bone, well-developed muscles and unexpectedly large weight for its size. Together with a rounded head, expressive eyes and charming expression, this represents a very special cat, unlike any other breed. Impeccable physical condition with excellent muscle tone. There should be no signs of obesity, abdominal pain, weakness, or apathy.
EARS AND EYES: The head is nicely rounded, without flat surfaces as seen from the front and from the side. The filled muzzle with a significant distance between the eyes smoothly passes into a wide, well developed, short muzzle that does not violate the rounded contours of the head. In the profile there is a pronounced transition from the forehead to the nose (visible nose break). The chin is strong, rounded, reflecting the proper bite. The neck is well developed. Ears are medium in size, planted at a good distance from each other, wide at the base and rounded at the tips. Leaning slightly forward, the ears contribute to the lively shape of the Burmese. The eyes are large, set at a great distance from each other, with a rounded contour and green color.
BODY: Medium in size, well muscled, compact in appearance. For cats we allow a larger size. A wide rounded thorax, a straight line of back from the shoulders to the tail. Well built, muscular.
LIMBS: In good proportion to the body.
FEET: Round. With characteristic white "socks" Five fingers on the front paw, four on the back.
TAIL: Fluffy, uniform and dark in color.
COAT: Thick and soft, requires a weekly combing.
DISADVANTAGES: Pronounced bands from the outside on the front or hind legs. Slightly pronounced traces of bands are acceptable in kittens and young adults. Elongated, substantially narrowed muzzle, leading to a wedge-shaped head, which reduces the roundness of the contours of the head. Green eyes. Incorrect tail.
DISQUALIFYING FAULTS: Broken tail, medallions or spots. Blue eyes. Strabismus. Incorrect color of the nose or paw pads. An anomaly of the occlusion, resulting in a pronounced snack or undershot, which visually violates the profile described in the standard. Incorrect jaw formation leading to protruding protruding teeth or to a skewed face or jaw. Expressed stripes on the body. Any color that differs from four acceptable colors: sable, champagne, blue and platinum.
General impression: Burmese differs from other breeds in its unique color, which is caused by the gene (cbcb). A striking characteristic of this bright breed is silky wool and expressive eyes. These are medium sized elegant cats. Any resemblance to the Siamese or stocky British type should be considered a drawback. The size is average.
Head: Front view: a short wedge, broad in cheekbones, tapering to a dull muzzle. The top of the head is wide and slightly rounded between widely spaced ears. In profile: the forehead is slightly rounded; The tip of the nose and chin form a vertical line. Nose: pronounced stop in base, jaws: powerful, strong chin, strong lower jaw visible in profile. Neck: Short, broad, muscular.
